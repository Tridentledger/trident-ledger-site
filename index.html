FAQ

Answers to the questions we hear most.

What's the difference between a fractional controller and a bookkeeper?

A bookkeeper records transactions and keeps your books up to date. A controller interprets those numbers, manages the close process, builds forecasts, and provides the financial leadership to drive decisions. Think of it this way: a bookkeeper tells you what happened. A controller tells you what it means and what to do next.

How much does this cost?

Every engagement is scoped to fit your business. Most clients invest between $3,000 and $8,000 per month depending on complexity, volume, and the level of strategic support they need. We'll give you a clear, fixed quote after the discovery call — no hidden fees, no hourly surprises.

How many hours per month do I need?

It depends on your business. Some clients need 15–20 hours a month for close and reporting. Others need 30–40+ hours for deeper financial planning and team oversight. We'll recommend the right level during the discovery call — and we can always adjust as your needs change.

Will you work with my existing accounting team?

Absolutely. That's one of the things we do best. We'll manage and mentor your bookkeepers, set up quality controls, and fill the gaps in your current team — not replace people who are doing good work.

What accounting software do you support?

We work with QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, NetSuite, Xero, and Sage. If you're on a different platform, let's talk — our team has worked across most major systems and can adapt quickly.

How quickly can we get started?

Most engagements are up and running within 1–2 weeks of signing the agreement. If your books are in rough shape, we may start with a cleanup phase before moving into ongoing services. Either way, you'll see progress fast.

Do we replace your CPA?

No — and we'll make their life a lot easier. Trident Ledger is not a CPA firm, but we work closely with your tax preparer to make sure they have clean, organized financials at year-end. Many of our clients tell us their CPA fees went down after working with us because the books are in much better shape.

What if my books are a mess?

That's not a dealbreaker — it's actually one of the most common starting points. We'll assess the damage, put together a cleanup plan, and get your books in shape before transitioning to ongoing monthly services. It's what we do.
